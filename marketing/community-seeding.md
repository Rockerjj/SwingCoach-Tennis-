# Community Seeding Playbook

## Rule #1: Provide Value First, Promote Second

Never lead with "download my app." Build credibility by being genuinely helpful in tennis communities. The app promotion should feel like a natural recommendation, not spam.

---

## Reddit Strategy

### Target Subreddits
- **r/tennis** (1.2M+ members) — General tennis discussion, news, technique
- **r/10s** (100K+ members) — Recreational/amateur tennis, form checks, gear
- **r/tennistips** — Technique-focused, form improvement
- **r/tennislessons** — Learning resources

### Approach

**Phase 1 (Week 1-2): Build karma and credibility**
- Comment helpfully on form-check posts with genuine technique advice
- Answer questions about stroke mechanics
- Share interesting observations about professional technique
- DO NOT mention the app yet

**Phase 2 (Week 2-3): Soft introduction**
- Post a form-check video of yourself, analyzed by the app
- Frame it as: "I've been using AI to analyze my strokes — here's what it found"
- Answer follow-up questions honestly, including what app you used
- Share a "I built something" post in r/10s explaining the app's origin story

**Phase 3 (Week 3+): Ongoing engagement**
- When someone posts a form-check video, offer to run it through AI and share results
- Respond to "how do I improve my [stroke]" posts with AI analysis insights
- Post periodic progress updates: "Month 2 of using AI coaching"

### Sample Reddit Posts

**Form Check Post:**
```
Title: Used AI to analyze my serve — found 3 things I never noticed

Body: I've been working on my serve for months and felt stuck. Started 
using an app that does AI pose analysis (Tennis Coach AI) and it flagged 
my toss placement, contact height, and follow-through angle. Here's the 
before/after with skeleton overlay. [images]

Would love feedback from this community too — does the AI analysis 
match what you see?
```

**Origin Story Post (for r/10s):**
```
Title: I'm a tennis player who built an AI coaching app — AMA

Body: I love tennis but can't afford a private coach every week. So I 
built an app that uses Apple Vision for pose estimation and GPT-4o to 
analyze strokes. It gives you skeleton overlays, letter grades, and 
specific coaching cues. Free to try (3 analyses).

Not trying to spam — genuinely curious if others find this useful. 
What would you want an AI tennis coach to tell you?
```

## Local Club Strategy

### Finding Clubs
- Search USTA facility finder for clubs near you
- Contact 5-10 clubs within driving distance
- Look for clubs with active adult leagues

### Offer
```
Subject: Free AI Tennis Coaching for Your Members

Hi [Club Name],

I built a free iOS app called Tennis Coach AI that uses AI to analyze 
tennis strokes and provide coaching feedback. I'd love to offer your 
members free premium access for 30 days.

Here's how it works:
- Players film their strokes with their iPhone
- The app uses Apple Vision to track their body mechanics
- AI provides letter grades and specific coaching cues

I'm looking for feedback from real tennis players to improve the app. 
Would you be open to sharing this with your members?

Happy to demo it in person if helpful.

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Follow-up at Club
- Offer to set up a "Try AI Coaching" station at the club
- Film a few members and show them their analysis on the spot
- Leave promo cards with QR code to download
- Ask the pro shop if they'd mention it to students

## Tennis Coach Outreach

### Finding Coaches
- USPTA and PTR coach directories
- Instagram search: #tenniscoach #tennispro #tennisinstructor
- YouTube: tennis coaching channels
- Local club pro shops

### Outreach Template (DM or Email)
```
Subject: Free AI tennis analysis tool for your students

Hi [Coach Name],

I built an iOS app called Tennis Coach AI that uses AI to analyze strokes 
with skeleton overlays and letter grades. I think it could be a great 
supplement to your lessons.

I'd love to offer you free lifetime premium access. If you find it 
useful, your students might benefit from using it between sessions to 
practice what you teach them.

No obligation — just want to get it in the hands of people who know 
tennis. Would you be open to trying it?

[Link to app]

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Why Coaches Are Valuable
- Each coach has 10-50+ students who trust their recommendations
- If a coach recommends the app, students download it
- Coaches can validate the AI's feedback, building credibility
- "Recommended by tennis coaches" is powerful social proof
